# User Manual

## How to Play (Quick Start)

1. **Watch the village open like a tiny video.** The first screen is a full-viewport layered painting: the camera eases in, clouds drift, boats bob, cafe cats serve, warehouse work moves, and depth layers separate with mouse/scroll parallax. The flat reference image is not used as the hero.
2. **Click Start Village Shift.** Optional: click **How to Play** for the visual in-game manual.
3. **Pick a difficulty and meet your crew** on the setup screen. Beginner is the gentlest, Expert is the noisiest; you can also rename the five cats and pick their accent colors.
4. **Enter the city.** The map is alive: cafe tips and location XP tick in on their own every couple of seconds, even before you decide anything. Watch for floating `+$` and `+XP` popups.
5. **Read the weekly Situation** on the Decision screen — a short story dilemma (a wedding order, a rival bakery, a tired crew...) with three choices, each with a different trade-off. Pick one before confirming the week.
6. **Check the Weekly Goal** card above it — a specific target (e.g. "keep bullwhip ratio under 1.8") that pays a cash bonus if you hit it.
7. **Forecast demand and place an order.** Use the suggested numbers as a safe default, or trust your own read of the trend. Pick one Village Action card to spend cash on a tactical edge for the week (or Steady Planner to spend nothing).
8. **Confirm the week.** You'll see the outcome: demand vs. forecast, profit, bonuses/penalties, your situation's payoff, whether you hit the weekly goal, a star rating (0–3 ⭐) and a letter grade (S/A/B/C/D) stamped on the result.
9. **Visit the map between decisions.** Click any location to see its status, run a Location Activity (a deeper strategic choice for that department), or launch its Field Command (an expensive, high-impact emergency move). Both grant XP toward that location's level.
10. **Level up locations.** Each of the 6 locations (Farm, Warehouse, Cafe, Customer District, Port, Analytics) levels independently from 1 to 5 through XP earned by real-time ticks, activities, commands, and weekly performance. Every level grants a permanent perk — cheaper purchasing, lower holding costs, better prices, calmer demand, fewer delays, or a tamer bullwhip ratio.
11. **Chase achievements.** A Trophy Case (visible on the Dashboard and Final Report) tracks 8 unlockable badges for milestones like your first profitable week, a perfect service week, or a 3-week stability streak.
12. **Repeat weekly** until the season ends, then check the Final Report for your rank, stats, and full Trophy Case — export a CSV or HTML report if you want the full data trail.

**The core loop:** watch the city earn passively → make one meaningful decision a week → level up locations → unlock perks and badges → keep the bullwhip calm → get a great final rank.

## Goal

Complete the season while keeping the village supply chain profitable and stable. You win by maintaining a strong balanced score based on profit, service level, customer satisfaction, and bullwhip control.

## Gameplay Flow

1. Start from the cinematic opening screen.
2. Choose a difficulty on the setup screen.
3. Personalize the five SCM characters if desired.
4. Explore the live, clickable city map — location levels and cash tick up in real time.
5. Click a location and run a location activity, or launch its field command for a dramatic intervention.
6. Open the decision screen each week.
7. Resolve the weekly Situation card (a story dilemma with three choices).
8. Check the Weekly Goal for a bonus target.
9. Enter a demand forecast.
10. Enter an order quantity.
11. Choose one village action card.
12. Confirm the week.
13. Read the consequence reveal: star rating, letter grade, bonuses, penalties, situation outcome, story branch, level-ups, and character reactions.
14. Review the dashboard, including the Trophy Case.
15. Continue until the final report screen appears.

## Location Activities

- Supplier Farm: choose Cheap Batch, Reliable Batch, or Flexible Batch.
- Central Warehouse: choose FIFO Focus, Safety Stock Wall, or Fast Pick Zone.
- Lavender Cafe: choose Calm Shelf, Flash Bundle, or Premium Display.
- Customer District: choose Social Buzz, Preorders, Foot Traffic, or Miso Weighted Signal.
- Port/Transport Hub: choose Fast Bridge, Safe Canal, Cheap Road, or Emergency Courier.
- Analytics Office: choose Smooth Forecast, Trend Forecast, Reactive Forecast, or Manual Forecast.

Each activity changes multiple variables and creates a Cause → Effect card. Repeating the same location activity in one week costs more.

## Village Action Cards

- Steady Planner: no extra cost; useful when the chain is stable.
- Expedite Cart: reduces next shipment lead time by one week but costs cash.
- Quality Tasting: blocks supplier quality loss for the current week.
- Cafe Specials: smooths sudden demand spikes and lowers demand noise.
- Shelf Prep: improves satisfaction when service level stays healthy.

## Weekly Situations

Every week a character brings you a short narrative dilemma tied to their department — a wedding preorder, a visiting food critic, a rival bakery cart, a tired crew, a bridge repair notice, and more. Each situation offers three choices with different trade-offs (cash cost, demand, prices, trust, route stability, operations heat). Your pick shapes that week's simulation and is narrated in the weekly result.

## Weekly Goals

Each week presents one rotating goal (e.g. Service Star, Whip Tamer, Sharp Forecast, Lean Shelves, Profitable Week, Calm Orders) with a cash reward if you hit it. Goal results appear as a banner on the weekly result screen.

## Location Leveling

Every location on the map — Supplier Farm, Warehouse, Cafe, Customer District, Port, Analytics Office — has its own level from 1 to 5, shown as an `L#` badge on the map and a full XP bar on the location panel. XP comes from:

- Real-time city ticks (ambient activity while you plan)
- Running that location's Location Activity (+18 XP)
- Launching that location's Field Command (+14 XP)
- Weekly performance tied to that location's specialty (e.g. the Cafe gains XP from service level, Analytics from a tame bullwhip ratio)

Each level grants a permanent perk that stacks with every other system in the game:

| Location | Perk per level |
|---|---|
| Supplier Farm | -3% purchase cost |
| Warehouse | -6% holding cost |
| Cafe | +2% selling price |
| Customer District | -5% demand noise |
| Port | -6% delay risk |
| Analytics Office | -5% bullwhip risk |

## Stability Streaks and Achievements

Consecutive weeks with high service level (90%+) and a low bullwhip ratio (under 2) build a stability streak, shown as a 🔥 chip with an escalating cash bonus. The Trophy Case (Dashboard and Final Report) tracks 8 achievements: First Profit, Full House, Whip Whisperer, Warm Streak, Golden Ledger, Goal Getter, Friend of Bean, and Three-Star Week.

## Field Commands

Click a map location, then launch its command from the location panel. These are high-impact moves inspired by strategy-game command centers:

- Supplier Farm: Harvest Surge adds a large near-term shipment.
- Central Warehouse: Reserve Release adds immediate usable inventory.
- Lavender Cafe: Preorder Push increases next demand and satisfaction.
- Customer District: Demand Smoothing reduces next-week demand noise.
- Port/Transport Hub: Route Reroute accelerates open shipments and blocks delay impact.
- Analytics Office: Miso Control Room reduces volatility and shields delay/quality risk.

Field commands cost cash and raise or lower operations heat, so use them intentionally.

## Story Branches and Final Rank

The final Balanced Kingdom Score combines profit, service level, satisfaction, bullwhip control, village reputation, supplier trust, customer reputation, and route stability.

Final ranks include Lavender Legend, Stable Chain Master, Cozy Operations Captain, Festival Survivor, Bullwhip Trouble, and Crisis Recovery Trainee.

Each week also earns its own star rating (0-3 ⭐) and letter grade (S/A/B/C/D) based on that week's balanced score — visible immediately on the weekly result screen, and totaled on the final report.

## Difficulty Levels

- Beginner: lower demand volatility, shorter lead times, fewer events, more starting inventory and cash.
- Medium: balanced challenge for normal gameplay.
- Hard: higher volatility, more delays, more expensive stockouts.
- Very Analytical / Expert: long season, noisy demand, high penalties, tight starting resources, and frequent disruptions.

## Important SCM Metrics

- Forecast Error = Actual Demand - Forecast Demand
- Absolute Forecast Error = abs(Actual Demand - Forecast)
- Service Level = Fulfilled Demand / Actual Demand
- Holding Cost = Ending Inventory × Holding Cost Rate
- Stockout Cost = Unfulfilled Demand × Stockout Penalty
- Bullwhip Ratio = Variance of Orders / Variance of Demand
- Profit = Revenue - Ordering Cost - Purchase Cost - Holding Cost - Stockout Cost - Disruption Cost
- Balanced Score = weighted score using profit, service level, bullwhip control, satisfaction, and stockout performance

## Strategy Tips

- Avoid reacting too strongly to one demand spike.
- Watch open pipeline orders before placing another large order.
- Keep enough inventory to protect service level, but avoid excessive holding cost.
- Use the dashboard to compare demand variance and order variance.
- A bullwhip ratio above 2 means orders are becoming much more volatile than customer demand.
- In harder modes, lead time uncertainty makes early planning more important.
