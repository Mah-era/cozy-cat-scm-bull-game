SECTION 01 · PLAYER VIEW

Overview

You are the planner of Lavender Cat Village's supply chain. Each week you forecast demand, place an order upstream, and resolve a story dilemma — while the city earns tips and XP in real time between your decisions. Over-react to noisy demand and the bullwhip cracks: orders swing wider than demand, costs pile up, and the crew gets stressed.

SECTION 02 · PLAYER VIEW

How to Play

  1. Launch the game. Run the commands on the right, open the printed URL, and click ☕ Start Village Shift (this also starts the music — turn sound on!).
  2. Pick a difficulty. Beginner (12 gentle weeks) → Very Analytical / Expert (24 noisy, tight-margin weeks).
  3. Meet the crew. Optionally rename Miso, Luna, Theo, Ivy & Noah and re-tint their fur.
  4. Watch the city live. On the map, cafe tips (+$) and location +XP popups tick in every couple of seconds — before you decide anything.
  5. Resolve the weekly Situation on the Decision screen — a three-choice story dilemma with real simulation trade-offs.
  6. Check the Weekly Goal (e.g. “keep bullwhip under 1.8”) — hit it for a cash bonus.
  7. Forecast & order. Enter forecast demand and order quantity (suggestions provided), pick one Village Action card, and Confirm Week.
  8. Read the result: star rating ⭐, letter grade (S–D) stamped on the panel, bonuses/penalties, situation outcome, and the crew's reactions.
  9. Level your city. Location Activities (+18 XP) and Field Commands (+14 XP) push districts toward Lv5 perks.
  10. Finish the season and collect your rank on the Final Report — then export CSV/HTML for analysis.

SECTION 03 · PLAYER VIEW

Beginner Guide

⏱First 5 minutes

Start on Beginner. Stay on the Map for ~30s and just watch: tips at the cafe, XP popups, cats breathing. Click each location to read its status, then open the Decision tab.

🎯First objective

Week 1: accept the suggested forecast and order, pick any Situation choice, keep Steady Planner, confirm. You'll almost certainly earn stars and the First Profit badge.

💰Core actions

Your two numbers each week are forecast and order. Everything else — activities, commands, situations — bends risk around those two numbers.

🆙Unlocking

Location levels unlock passively; nudge favorites with that location's Activity (+18 XP). Achievements unlock from play — Friend of Bean just needs a kind heart when the stray appears.

🧭When lost

Read Miso's note (purple card, bottom of most panels) — the advisor cat comments on exactly what went wrong and suggests next week's number.

⚡Progress faster

Chase the Weekly Goal every week and keep service ≥90% with bullwhip <2 — the 🔥 streak bonus compounds, and stars fill the header counter.

SECTION 05 · PLAYER VIEW

Core Systems

🌊Bullwhip engine

Every week the game computes order variance ÷ demand variance. Chase one demand spike with a giant order and the ratio — and your costs — amplify upstream. The whole game is built to make you feel this.

⏱Real-time city ticks

Every ~2.1s: cafe tips (scaled by satisfaction) flow into cash, ambient events grant location XP, and floating popups rise off the map. Level-ups interrupt with fanfare + confetti.

🆙Location leveling

Six districts, independent XP tracks (60→460 XP thresholds), Lv1→Lv5. Each level is a permanent perk: −3% purchase cost, −6% holding, +2% price, −5% noise, −6% delay, −5% bullwhip.

📦Pipeline & lead time

Orders arrive 1–7 weeks later depending on difficulty, route choice, supplier trust, heat, and luck. Count what's already in the pipeline before ordering more — or drown in holding cost.

🎭Weekly situations

Eight narrative dilemmas (wedding orders, food critics, rival croissant carts, a stray cat named Bean) — three choices each, wired straight into the economy.

🃏Actions & commands

Five weekly action cards (Expedite Cart, Quality Tasting…) plus six per-district Field Commands (Harvest Surge, Route Reroute, Miso Control Room) for emergencies.

⭐Stars, grades, streaks

Each week scores a 0–3⭐ rating and an S/A/B/C/D ink-stamp grade. Calm, high-service weeks chain into a 🔥 streak with escalating cash bonuses.

🏆Trophy case

8 achievements from First Profit to Whip Whisperer (bullwhip < 1.3) and Friend of Bean. Visible on Dashboard + Final Report.

🎵Procedural audio

A synthesized lo-fi loop (Fmaj7–Am7–Dm7–G7) plus coins, stamps, alarms, fanfares and one very important meow — zero audio files shipped, with separate 🎵/🔊 mute toggles. Hear every cue in the Audio Lab.

🌸Cozy maximalism

Nearly every element idles in motion: cards float on staggered phases, panels breathe, chips bob, badges rock, buttons sheen, petals and sparkles drift across the whole app — paused instantly on hover and disabled under reduced-motion.

SECTION 06 · PLAYER VIEW · FOR SCM PEOPLE

How the Features Teach the Real Bullwhip Effect

The Bullwhip Effect is the classic supply-chain failure mode: small wiggles in customer demand become huge, expensive swings in upstream orders. Every "fun" feature in this game is secretly one of its real causes or cures — the same dynamics taught with the MIT Beer Game, but with dopamine instead of worksheets.

Game feature (the fun)Real SCM concept (the lesson)What you feel
📈 Bullwhip ratio KPI + alert meterVariance amplification: Var(orders) / Var(demand) — the textbook bullwhip measure (Lee, Padmanabhan & Whang)Panic-order after one spike and watch the ratio jump above 2 — then pay for it for weeks. The whip is your own hand.
📦 Shipment pipeline + lead timesOrder-to-delivery delay — the #1 bullwhip driver in the Beer GameOrders placed in week 3 land in week 6. Forget what's already in transit and you double-order; the game punishes it with holding cost.
🔮 Forecast input + smoothing suggestionDemand-signal processing; exponential/weighted-average smoothing vs. naive reactive forecastingMiso's suggested number is a weighted moving average. Chase last week's spike instead and your forecast error KPI exposes you.
🎭 Weekly Situations (promos, critics, weddings)Demand shaping, price fluctuation & order batching — Lee's classic bullwhip causes wrapped in storyA "Flash Bundle" promo feels great until the shelf empties — exactly how real promotions inject artificial demand variance.
🤝 Supplier trust & route stability metersSupply-side reliability: shortage gaming, rationing, and relationship capitalPanic orders and cheap contracts erode trust → delays rise → you order even more. The vicious cycle, felt as sad cat faces.
🆙 Location levels & perksContinuous-improvement investments: cheaper procurement, lower holding, calmer signals, faster routesLeveling Analytics literally reduces bullwhip risk −5%/level — process maturity as a progression system.
🎯 Weekly goals + ⭐ stars + S–D gradesBalanced-scorecard KPIs: service level, forecast accuracy, inventory turns, cost controlYou can't max profit by ignoring service — the balanced score forces the real multi-objective trade-off planners live with.
🔥 Stability streaksThe value of steady-state operations over heroicsThe biggest bonuses come from being boring — the deepest SCM lesson in the game.
🚨 Field commands (Harvest Surge, Courier…)Expediting & emergency procurement — expensive, heat-raising last resortsThey work, once — then operations heat and cost teach you why firefighting isn't a strategy.
📊 Demand-vs-Orders chartThe bullwhip made visible: the purple order line swinging wider than the green demand lineOne glance at the final-report chart shows a whole season of your over-reactions. It's the Beer Game debrief, automated.
📤 CSV / HTML exportPost-game analysis: full weekly ledger with variances, costs, service, and scoresDrop it in a spreadsheet, compute your own ratios, run a classroom debrief.
🎓 Classroom use: run Beginner as a guided intro, Expert for a noisy-signal challenge. Compare exported CSVs across students — whoever kept order variance closest to demand variance internalized the lesson. The game never says "read this theory"; it makes you pay cash for every classic mistake.

🎧 The lo-fi loop is a Fmaj7 → Am7 → Dm7 → G7 progression: warm sine pads, a humanized triangle arpeggio (notes randomly skipped for feel), and a soft bass pluck on beats 1 and 3 — one 3.6-second bar per chord, forever. The preview button plays two bars.

SECTION 15 · DEVELOPER VIEW

Architecture

The defining decision is the dual-clock model: a deliberate, turn-based weekly resolver layered under a passive real-time dopamine engine — both producing immutable state snapshots consumed by the same React tree.

main.jsxmounts <App/> + styles.css
gameContent.jscharacters · activities · situations · story text
audio.js / juice.jsxSFX & music · confetti · count-ups · toasts
▼ imports ▼
App.jsx — phase machinestart → setup → game (Map / Decision / Result / Dashboard / Final)
simulation.js — weekly clocksimulateWeek(state, decision) → new state (economy, bullwhip, XP, achievements)
simulation.js — real-time clockcityTick(state) every ~2.1s → new state + popup events
▼ state → render ▼
UI event / interval → pure state fn → setGame(next) → React re-render → SVG map · charts · cats · popups → sfx + confetti side-effects

Weekly resolution pipeline (simulateWeek)

  1. Merge modifiers: activity effects + situation choice + location-level perks fold into one multiplier set.
  2. Roll event → compute demand: base + trend + damped noise × event multiplier.
  3. Advance pipeline: arrivals, quality loss, emergency procurement, fulfillment, stockouts.
  4. Cost stack: purchase, holding, stockout, ordering, disruption, action, emergency, situation cash.
  5. KPIs: service level, forecast error, demand/order variance → bullwhip ratio.
  6. Meters & heat: supplier trust, reputations, patience, route stability, operations heat.
  7. Rewards: bonuses/penalties, weekly goal, streak, stars ⭐ + S–D grade, per-location XP, achievements, story branch, Miso advice.
🧠 Gotcha: the tick interval reads game state through a gameRef (a ref mirroring the latest state) so the setInterval never closes over stale state and never needs re-creating — the classic React stale-closure trap, avoided.
